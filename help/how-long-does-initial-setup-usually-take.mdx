# How long does initial setup usually take?

Initial setup usually takes anywhere from a few minutes to a few days, depending on how much you need to configure. Most teams can complete the basics in one sitting and then refine workflows, permissions, and integrations over time.

## Typical setup timeline

If you are creating an account, connecting core tools, and setting up a simple first workflow, setup is usually quick. Larger teams or more complex onboarding plans often need extra time for approvals, integrations, testing, and internal alignment.

<Callout kind="info">
Timing varies based on your team size, the number of integrations you need, and how customized your setup needs to be.
</Callout>

## Common setup phases

<Steps>
  <Step title="Account creation and workspace setup">
    Create your account, confirm your email, and configure your workspace basics. For most customers, this part only takes a few minutes.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Initial configuration">
    Add team members, review settings, and connect the tools you plan to use first. This often takes a bit longer if you need admin access or approval from another team.
  </Step>

  <Step title="First workflow or use case">
    Build your first workflow, test it, and make small adjustments. This is usually the longest phase because it depends on how clearly your process is defined.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Team rollout and refinement">
    After the first setup is working, most teams spend additional time refining permissions, naming conventions, and workflow details. This phase is optional for getting started, but it helps long-term adoption.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What speeds setup up

A few things usually make onboarding faster:

- Know your first use case before you begin
- Start with one workflow instead of trying to configure everything at once
- Have access to the apps, accounts, or tools you want to connect
- Invite the right teammates early if approvals or shared access are required

## What can slow setup down

Setup usually takes longer when:

- You need multiple integrations configured at once
- Your team requires security, legal, or admin review
- You are migrating existing processes from another system
- Your workflow needs testing across several departments

## Need help getting set up?

If setup is taking longer than expected, focus on one simple workflow first and add complexity later. If you still need help, contact support or review related Help Center articles for step-by-step guidance.
