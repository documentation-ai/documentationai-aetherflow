# What should I do if a page is not loading?

If a page is not loading, start with quick browser checks before assuming there is a larger issue. Most loading problems come from a temporary browser problem, a local connection issue, or a broader service interruption.

<Callout kind="info">

Start with the fastest checks first:

- Refresh the page.
- Confirm your internet connection is working.
- Open the page in an incognito window.
- Try another browser.
- If the problem continues, check whether your team is seeing the same issue.

</Callout>

## Check your browser and connection

A page may fail to load when your browser has stale data, an extension interferes with the session, or your internet connection drops briefly.

First, make sure other websites open normally in the same browser. If other sites are also slow or unavailable, the issue is likely your connection rather than a single page.

If only one page is affected, try loading it again after a short wait. Temporary network interruptions or browser session problems often resolve on the next attempt.

## Try isolation steps

Use these steps to find out whether the problem is tied to your current browser session.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Refresh the page">
  
  Reload the page once to rule out a temporary loading error. If the page opens after refresh, the issue was likely a brief session or network interruption.
  
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the page in an incognito window">
  
  An incognito window helps rule out cached files, cookies, or browser extensions that may be interfering. If the page loads there, the issue is likely related to your regular browser session.
  
  </Step>
  <Step title="Try another browser">
  
  Open the same page in a different browser on the same device. If it works in another browser, focus on the original browser's cache, extensions, or settings.
  
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Check for a broader service issue

If the page still does not load after trying those steps, check whether other people on your team see the same behavior. A shared issue across multiple users usually points to a broader service interruption rather than a problem on your device.

If only you are affected, continue troubleshooting your browser or connection. If multiple people are affected, wait for service to recover or contact your internal team if they manage access and network settings.

If the problem continues after these checks, contact support and include what you tried, whether the issue happens in multiple browsers, and whether other people on your team are affected.
