# Can we set advanced permissions for different teams?

Enterprise workspaces usually support role-based access controls, so admins can give different teams the level of access they need.

<Callout kind="info">

Enterprise workspaces usually support role-based access controls so admins can manage who can view, edit, approve, or manage workflows and settings.

</Callout>

## What admins can control

Admins can usually use role-based access controls to decide which people or teams can access specific parts of the workspace. This helps separate day-to-day work from workspace administration.

Common permission areas include:

- **View** workflows and workspace content
- **Edit** workflows
- **Approve** workflow changes or related actions
- **Manage workflows** across teams
- **Manage settings** for the workspace

## How to organize permissions across teams

Set permissions based on what each team needs to do. Give the broadest access only to the people responsible for managing workflows and workspace settings.

For example, you might give one team permission to view and edit workflows, while limiting approval or settings access to admins or team leads. This keeps access aligned with team responsibilities without giving every team the same level of control.
