# Can I invite teammates during setup?

Yes. You can invite teammates during setup if you want to split tasks, review settings together, or make sure the right people have access from the start.

<Callout kind="info">

Invite only the people you need for the first setup pass. Before you send invites, decide who should be an admin, who only needs workspace access, and who can wait until setup is complete.

</Callout>

## Invite teammates during setup

Use setup to bring in teammates who need to help configure your workspace or verify key settings.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your team or workspace settings" icon="settings">

  Sign in to your account, then go to the area where team members, users, or workspace access is managed.

  If your setup checklist includes account or organization settings, you will usually find invitations there.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Add each teammate’s email address" icon="users">

  Enter the work email address for each person you want to invite.

  Double-check the address before sending. A correct address helps your teammate receive the invitation without delays.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the right access level" icon="shield">

  Assign the lowest level of access each person needs to do their part of setup.

  For example, give admin access only to people who need to manage billing, security, or workspace-wide settings.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invitations" icon="arrow-right">

  Review the list of invited teammates, then send the invitations.

  Your teammates should receive an email with instructions to join your workspace.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm they joined and completed their task" icon="check-circle">

  After each teammate accepts, ask them to complete the part of setup they own, such as connecting a tool, reviewing permissions, or checking notifications.

  You know this step is complete when the teammate appears in your member list and can access the areas they need.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## Who to invite first

Start with the people who unblock setup for everyone else. That usually means inviting a small group first instead of the whole team.

- **Workspace owner or team lead** — approves key decisions and keeps setup moving.
- **Admin or IT contact** — handles permissions, security settings, and account-level access.
- **Operational owner** — confirms workflows, team structure, or day-to-day setup choices.
- **Billing contact** — reviews plan, payment details, or subscription ownership if needed.

Wait to invite broader teams until the core setup is done. That keeps your workspace organized and reduces confusion while settings are still changing.

## Why inviting early helps

Inviting the right teammates during setup can save time later. You can review settings together, avoid handoff delays, and make sure ownership is clear before people start using the workspace.

It also helps prevent rework. If billing, permissions, and workflow decisions are confirmed early, you are less likely to update access or settings after rollout.

## Need help with the next step?

If you are unsure who should have admin access, start with one or two trusted leads and add others after setup is complete. Keep your initial group small, then expand access once your workspace structure is in place.
