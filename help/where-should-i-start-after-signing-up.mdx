# Where should I start after signing up?

Your first few minutes in AetherFlow should help you reach one small, useful win. Start by getting familiar with the workspace, connecting what you already use, and creating a simple workflow you can test right away.

## Recommended starting point

<Callout kind="info">

Before you begin, make sure you can log in to your account, access your dashboard, and confirm your email address if you were asked to verify it. If you already know one repetitive task you want to automate, keep that task in mind as you go through the steps below.

</Callout>

## Recommended first actions

<Steps>
  <Step title="Take a quick look around your dashboard">
  
  Open your main dashboard and scan the key areas, such as workflow creation, integrations, and account settings. This gives you a clear sense of where to return when you start building.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete your basic profile and workspace settings">
  
  Add your name, company details, and any core preferences your workspace asks for. Finishing these basics early makes later setup smoother, especially if you invite teammates or connect multiple tools.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect at least one integration">
  
  Connect the app or service you use most often, such as email, a spreadsheet, or a project management tool. Starting with one familiar integration makes it easier to build a workflow that feels immediately useful.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Create one simple workflow">
  
  Choose a small task with a clear result, such as sending a notification, updating a record, or moving information from one tool to another. A simple first workflow helps you learn the product without adding unnecessary setup.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a test and confirm the result">
  
  Test the workflow and check that the expected action happens in the connected app. If the workflow completes successfully, you now have a working baseline you can expand later.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## What success looks like

You are in a good place to keep going when a few things are true. You can log in without issues, your workspace details are set, at least one integration is connected, and your first workflow runs as expected.

That first workflow does not need to be complex. If it solves one real task and gives you a result you can verify, you have already built a strong starting point.

## Need help choosing your first workflow?

If you are not sure what to automate first, pick a task you repeat every day or every week. Good starting examples include sending internal alerts, updating a tracker, or moving new form submissions into the tool your team already uses.
