# Do I need technical experience to get started?

Many people get started without a technical background. If you can navigate a dashboard, connect apps, and follow guided setup steps, you can usually complete the basics on your own.

<Callout kind="info">
Many early setup tasks can be completed without deep technical expertise. Most new users begin by connecting apps, choosing templates, and turning on simple automations before involving an admin or developer.
</Callout>

## Beginner-friendly ways to start

The easiest way to begin is with simple, low-risk tasks that help you learn the product without changing complex systems. Start with actions that use built-in prompts, templates, or guided setup.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Sign up, log in, and take a quick look around the dashboard so you know where your main tools and settings are.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a simple use case">
    Pick one small task you already understand, such as sending a notification, organizing incoming requests, or updating a record after a form submission.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use a template or guided setup">
    Start with a pre-built workflow or setup guide instead of building from scratch. This helps you learn the basic flow before making custom changes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect one app at a time">
    Add a single integration and test it before connecting anything else. A successful first connection is a good sign that you are ready for the next step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a small test">
    Trigger the workflow with sample data and confirm the expected result appears in your dashboard or connected app. Once that works, you can expand with more confidence.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## When technical help may be useful

Some setups are straightforward, but a few situations usually benefit from help from an admin, IT contact, or developer. That support can save time and prevent configuration issues later.

Technical input may help when you need to:

- Set up custom domains, DNS records, or email authentication
- Connect internal tools, private systems, or databases
- Configure advanced permissions, security policies, or user access controls
- Work with APIs, webhooks, or custom scripts
- Troubleshoot network restrictions, single sign-on, or company-managed environments

If your team already has an admin or technical owner, involve them early for these cases. You can still handle the day-to-day product setup while they support the parts that depend on your company's systems.

## What to do next

Start with one simple workflow and build from there. Small early wins make the rest of setup much easier.
