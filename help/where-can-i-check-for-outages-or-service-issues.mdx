# Where can I check for outages or service issues?

Service issues are easiest to confirm by checking official status sources before troubleshooting your own setup. If an incident is already known, you can usually save time and avoid repeating steps that will not fix the problem.

<Callout kind="info">

Check known incidents first. Review your status page, service notifications, and support updates before assuming the issue is isolated to your account.

</Callout>

## Check official status sources

Start with your public status page. Status pages usually show active incidents, degraded performance, maintenance windows, and recovery progress.

If the issue affects multiple users or a core service, the status page is usually the fastest place to confirm it. Look for details such as the affected service, the time the incident started, and whether a fix is already in progress.

## Review notifications and support updates

If the status page does not show a clear incident, check any service notifications you receive through email, in-app alerts, or your support portal. Some disruptions appear there first, especially when the issue affects a smaller group of users or a specific feature.

Support updates can also clarify whether the team is already investigating similar reports. Compare the timing of your issue with any recent notices so you can tell whether your problem matches a known event.

## If no incident is listed

If you cannot find a reported outage, gather a few details before contacting support. That gives the support team enough context to check logs, compare reports, and confirm whether the issue is isolated or part of a wider problem.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Note the time the issue happened">

Record the date, time, and time zone for the problem. If the issue happened more than once, note the most recent occurrence and whether it is still happening now.

This helps support match your report to platform activity and any incident timelines already under review.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Capture the error details">

Write down the exact error message, failed action, or unexpected behavior you saw. Include any relevant IDs, screenshots, or the name of the feature or workflow involved if you have them.

Exact details are more useful than a summary because they help support distinguish between a known incident and a separate configuration problem.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Contact support with the information you collected">

Share the time of the issue and the error details in your support request. Mention that you already checked the status page, notifications, and support updates and did not find a matching incident.

That gives support a clear starting point and reduces back-and-forth before investigation begins.

  </Step>
</Steps>
